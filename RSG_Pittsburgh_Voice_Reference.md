# Rivers Casino Pittsburgh — Voice Reference

*Distilled from five months of finished creative work (Nov 2025 - Mar 2026). This is what the property sounds like, made explicit.*

## What Pittsburgh sounds like in one paragraph

Pittsburgh's voice lives in **platform identities and visual systems**, not in headline copy. Most headline lines are functional or transactional. The brand equity sits in the named sub-brands (Take It or Risk It Fridays, Pittsburgh Party Pit, Forever Fun Club, Wild & Crazy Cash, Now & Then Mustang) and in the recurring visual systems. When the team has done strong copy work at Pittsburgh, it has been seasonal moments inside the platform identity ("More is merrier" for the holiday Gift Card, "Now & Then" for the Mustang giveaway, "55 Winners of $55" for Forever Fun), not the platform itself.

## The Memphis Q1/Q2 quarterly theme

Pittsburgh runs **quarterly visual themes**. The other four RSG properties do not. The current theme (Q1/Q2 2026) is Memphis Group / 80s — cyan, pink, yellow, navy, geometric confetti, lightning bolts, lucky 7s, jagged triangles. Memphis is the implicit visual house style for ongoing offer work right now. New copy needs to live inside this visual world for Q1 and Q2.

## Pittsburgh's named platforms

Each of these has its own voice rules. Don't mix them.

**Take It or Risk It Fridays.** Tagline: "A new twist on Mystery Fridays." Body voice: "Take it or Risk it. Higher? Lower? The choice is YOURS!" Memphis pattern visual. This is Pittsburgh's adaptation of Philadelphia's Mystery Fridays platform. Strongest concept work at the property. Recurs monthly.

**Forever Fun Club** (55+). Yellow + blue slot-reel-style logo. Tagline: "Same 55+ Club, New Name!" Standard offer is FREE SLOT PLAY & DINING OFFER plus HOT SEAT DRAWINGS, five winners every half hour of $150 Free Slot Play. Tuesdays of the month. Cross-property platform — also at Philadelphia and Portsmouth.

**Wild & Crazy Cash.** Bold green field, distressed type, $100-bill imagery. Voice is shouty and functional. "$25,000 WILD & CRAZY CASH / TOP PRIZE OF $12,000 CASH / 5 TOTAL WINNERS." Money-and-green register.

**Now & Then Mustang Giveaway.** Two-vehicle giveaway: 2026 Ford Mustang and Classic Ford Mustang. Retro-modern striped backdrop (cream, red, teal, orange). Pun is in the platform name; copy supports it.

**Pittsburgh Party Pit.** Black + yellow, athletic equipment iconography (helmet, hockey stick, baseball bat). The most property-distinctive sub-brand in the corpus. Thursdays in March. "Table Games Hot Seat Drawing every 30 minutes / TOP PRIZE OF $1,000 CASH each Thursday."

**Mystery Gift Card / Sunday Mystery Scratch-Off.** Multiple gift-card brand logos with one ??? card. Functional headline pattern.

**$10,000 Jackpot Party / $30,000 High Limit Jackpot Party.** Standard recurring drawings. Last Wednesday of the month for $10K. Cross-property logo system.

**Gift Express (powered by Gift & Go).** House-icon-with-yellow-bow mark. "Claim your gift each week!" Cross-property platform.

## Pittsburgh's phrase library

Thinner than Des Plaines. The phrases that recur:

- "Last Wednesday of the Month" (Jackpot Party)
- "Win a taxable jackpot on any slot machine and receive an entry for a chance to win." (drawings body copy)
- "Claim your gift each week!" (Gift Express)
- "Higher? Lower? The choice is YOURS!" (Take It or Risk It)
- "Five Winners Every Half Hour" (Hot Seat formats)

That's most of it. Pittsburgh's voice is in the platforms more than the phrases.

## Local hooks Pittsburgh can credibly invoke

- **Pittsburgh Party Pit / Steelers + Pirates color palette.** Local pride is on the table for table-games promotions specifically.
- **NFL Draft (April 22-25, 2026).** Pittsburgh hosts. The "You're on the clock" framing is in use. Football imagery is appropriate. Genuine local-moment integration window.
- **Giant Eagle® Gift Card Giveaway.** The local Pittsburgh-area grocery chain is a recurring giveaway partner.
- **The Landing Hotel Pittsburgh.** On-site amenity, surfaces in core mailers and tier upgrades.

## What Pittsburgh doesn't do

- **Hockey-themed puns.** Those belong to Philadelphia (Flyers partnership).
- **Civic / community-impact register.** Not present in the corpus.
- **Multilingual / trilingual copy.** Not present in the corpus.
- **Pun-heavy headlines as a default.** Pittsburgh is not Des Plaines. The headlines do their work functionally and let the platform names carry the brand voice.
- **Em dashes.** Not in the brand's punctuation vocabulary.
- **Parallel declarative rhythm** ("Spin the reels. Win the prize. Make memories.") The corpus uses single fragments, comma-stitched phrases, question-pairs, or imperatives instead.

## Where Pittsburgh's craft ceiling has been

When the team has had time and a good seasonal hook, Pittsburgh has produced:

- "More is merrier" (Holiday Gift Card)
- "Now & Then" platform name (Mustang giveaway)
- "55 Winners of $55" (Forever Fun)
- "Higher? Lower? The choice is YOURS!" (Take It or Risk It)
- "Pittsburgh Party Pit" sub-brand naming (the table-games local-color play)

Pittsburgh's ceiling is not Des Plaines' ceiling. The platform-and-system voice is the right answer; the question is making sure the seasonal hooks get the attention they deserve when they show up.
