# Rivers Casino Des Plaines — Voice Reference

*Distilled from five months of finished creative work (Nov 2025 - Mar 2026). This is what the property sounds like, made explicit.*

## What Des Plaines sounds like in one paragraph

Des Plaines' voice lives in **headline copy**, not in visual systems. Visuals are photography-led with a navy-and-Rivers-gold brand wrap; there is no pattern equivalent to Pittsburgh's Memphis system. The copy is where the craft is. The dominant headline mode is **pun-on-mechanic** — the headline contains wordplay that ties the offer mechanic to the seasonal moment, the gift, the partner, or the format itself. The puns are tight, two-level, and unmistakably crafted. Des Plaines also has the strongest workhorse phrase library and the most brand-mature dining ecosystem of any RSG property.

## The pun-on-mechanic pattern

Look at how each of these connects the offer to a second meaning:

- "Cake, candles and a lucky year." — birthday slot bonus (birthday wishes plus slot luck)
- "Love at first deal." — Valentine's table play (Valentine's plus card-game wordplay)
- "Love at first spin." — Valentine's slot play (same template, slot variant)
- "Cloudy with a chance to win." — April Showers Drawing (weather idiom plus drawing mechanic)
- "Power up your play." — VIP gift giveaway when the gift was a portable charger
- "Gifts are a recipe for success." — April Gift Giveaway with cookware
- "Win! Place! Show!" — Derby Drawing (horse-racing terminology used as headline)
- "Play for points. Race for a million." — Black Card Premier Points Race plus Rush to $1 Million tentpole
- "Treat yourself." — Clean Out the Closet Giveaway (spring cleaning plus self-care)
- "Don't keep the thrills waiting." — workhorse pun on the workhorse phrase
- "Get ready to get away." / "Double up the vacation fun." — MSC Cruise + Atlantis travel partner
- "Come one, come all." — Big Top Pops (circus showmanship for the theatrical event)

The pattern: each pun connects the offer mechanic, the gift, the seasonal moment, the partner, or the format to a second well-known phrase. Both meanings are intended. The pun is short, tight, and unmistakably crafted. A pun without a second-meaning anchor isn't doing the work.

## Des Plaines' phrase library (the workhorses)

Use these where they fit. The library is real, recurring, and recognizable:

- **"Thrills await."** — slot play, default state
- **"Don't keep the thrills waiting."** — slot play, more urgent variant
- **"Weekends are for spinning!"** — weekend bonus slot play
- **"Let's get the weekend spinning!"** — weekend variant
- **"Rewards await."** — tier exclusives (Black Card, Platinum)
- **"Rewards bursting with bonuses."** — points bonus
- **"Pick your day to play."** / **"Pick your day to play!"** — multi-day or kiosk offers
- **"Delicious flavors await."** — Mian dining (restaurant-specific)
- **"Rewards you can taste."** — RC Supply Co. dining (restaurant-specific)

When an ongoing slot play DM doesn't have a strong seasonal hook, one of these is usually the right answer.

## Des Plaines' named platforms

**Rush to $1 Million.** Custom diamond mark. Multi-month tentpole campaign (April-June, finalist drawing late June, August finale). "Someone is guaranteed to win $100,000!" / "Rush in to play... for your chance to win." Earn one entry for every 500 Points; 3X entries on Mondays.

**Big Top Pops.** Custom mark with popcorn-bag illustration in the "O" of POPS. Theatrical / circus / symphony register. "Come one, come all. / Dinner worthy of the Center Ring. Plus, you could win a share of $10,000!"

**April Showers Drawing.** Custom mark with umbrella and flower petals. "Cloudy with a chance to win a share of $40,000!" Cross-property platform with Schenectady.

**Lunar New Year platform.** Year of the Horse Rush Rewards card. Vermillion + gold palette. "Happy Lunar New Year! / 春节快乐! / CHÚC MỪNG NĂM MỚI!" **Trilingual production** (English / Chinese / Vietnamese). Companion piece: Red Envelope Giveaway, "PHÁT LÌ XÌ MAY MẮN" Vietnamese subhead, $40,000 prize pool.

**$250,000 Jackpot Party.** Cross-property logo system at the DP scale. Recurring monthly. "WIN YOUR SHARE OF UP TO $250,000 / Be one of 10 lucky winners!"

**Cinco de Mayo Slot Play / Margarita Giveaway.** Cultural craft with papel picado motif (Mexican cut paper). Used purposefully, not decoratively.

**Derby Drawing.** Kentucky Derby tie-in. Custom gold script with horse silhouette. "Win! Place! Show!" Authentic horse-racing terminology used as headline.

**Diverse Supplier civic.** "Diversity equals success." Formal corporate register. MWPVBE supplier outreach. Distinct voice mode for civic work.

## Restaurant ecosystem (six distinct identities)

Des Plaines has the most brand-mature dining portfolio in the RSG corpus. Each restaurant has its own visual register and copy voice:

- **Mian** (麺) — Chinese-character mark, red type, dim/intimate restaurant photography. Voice: "Delicious flavors await."
- **RC Supply Co.** — circular green/navy roundel, refined plated-food photography. Voice: "Rewards you can taste."
- **Hugo's Frog Bar & Chop House** — golden serif type with custom dressed-up frog mascot, dramatic meat photography on dark wood. Steakhouse register.
- **Coffee Spot** — Starbucks-licensed location ("We Proudly Serve" badge). Cafe register.
- **Taco Suerte** — Mexican cuisine.
- **FLIPT** — burger-forward.

When writing for a specific restaurant, use that restaurant's register. Don't apply Mian's "Delicious flavors await" to Hugo's; the restaurants are not interchangeable.

## Local hooks Des Plaines can credibly invoke

- **Chicagoland and Des Plaines** as community references (used in Diverse Supplier ads: "throughout the Chicagoland and Des Plaines communities")
- **The trilingual Lunar New Year audience** (English / Chinese / Vietnamese) — culturally specific, with Year of the Horse iconography for 2026
- **The dining ecosystem** as a credible source of restaurant-specific creative
- **Civic / supplier-diversity register** for corporate-citizen work

## What Des Plaines doesn't do

- **Quarterly visual themes.** Not present at Des Plaines (only at Pittsburgh).
- **Pittsburgh-style platform-and-system functional copy as the default.** DP's voice is in the headline craft, not in platform names.
- **Hockey or NFL-specific local sports puns.** Those belong to Philadelphia and Pittsburgh respectively.
- **English-only Lunar New Year copy.** When you write for LNY at DP, flag that translation is needed. The property's LNY work is trilingual.
- **Em dashes.** Not in the brand's punctuation vocabulary.
- **Parallel declarative rhythm** ("Spin the reels. Win the prize. Make memories.") The corpus uses single fragments, comma-stitched phrases, question-pairs, or imperatives instead.
- **Punning for the sake of punning.** The puns work because they tie to the mechanic, gift, moment, partner, or format. A pun without a second-meaning anchor is empty.

## Where Des Plaines' craft ceiling has been

When the team has had time and a good seasonal hook, Des Plaines has produced:

- "Cloudy with a chance to win" (April Showers Drawing — strongest pun craft in the property)
- "Win! Place! Show!" (Derby Drawing — borrowed cadence used precisely)
- "Cake, candles and a lucky year" (Birthday DM)
- "Power up your play" (VIP charger gift)
- "Come one, come all. Dinner worthy of the Center Ring." (Big Top Pops)
- The trilingual Year of the Horse / Red Envelope coordinated platform work

The phrase library is doing structural work in DP that no other property has built. It's an asset other properties don't have.
