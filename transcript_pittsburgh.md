## Property summary observations (raw, not analysis)

- **Pittsburgh has TWO real visual systems doing the heavy lifting:** the Memphis Group geometric pattern (cyan/pink/yellow on navy + lightning bolts + lucky 7s + confetti) is the dominant ongoing system. It carries Take It or Risk It, Sunday Mystery Scratch-Off, Mystery Gift Card, Gift Express, Giant Eagle Gift Card, March Bonus, Slot Core Calendar, Table Core. The system is genuinely consistent across pieces. Second system: Pittsburgh Party Pit's black-and-yellow sports identity. Third: Forever Fun 55+ has its own slot-reel logo system that travels.
- **Pittsburgh has THREE genuinely property-distinctive moments in the corpus:** Pittsburgh Party Pit (Steelers/Pirates colors + sports iconography), "You're on the clock" NFL Draft tie-in, Giant Eagle gift card (regional grocery partner). Everything else could ship at any property.
- **Pittsburgh leans on platform/concept names heavily:** Take It or Risk It, Forever Fun, Wild & Crazy Cash, Now & Then, Mystery Gift Card, Sunday Mystery Scratch-Off, Party Pit, Pack the Pantry, $10K Jackpot Party. These are concept platforms with their own logos and are doing more brand work than the headlines themselves.
- **Headline copy register is functional with light wordplay where seasonal hooks allow:** "More is merrier" (Holiday), "Now & Then" (Mustang), "55 Winners of $55" (number pun, Forever Fun). Most copy is informational ("Win a taxable jackpot...", "Claim your gift each week!", "Higher? Lower? The choice is YOURS!"). No durable voice signature in the headline copy itself — the voice is in the platform names and visual systems.
- **Memphis pattern is acting as the implicit "Rivers Casino Pittsburgh ongoing voice."** It's not labeled or claimed anywhere I can see, but it appears on most non-tier, non-tier-upgrade work.
