## Property summary observations (raw, not analysis)

- **Des Plaines's primary visual system is photography-led, not pattern-led.** Lifestyle photography (people excited at slots, couples toasting, hands at table games), product photography (slot reels, Rush Rewards cards, food, gift items, vehicles), and a navy + blue + Rivers gold gradient as the wrap. No equivalent of the Pittsburgh quarterly theme. Each piece is its own visual moment.
- **Des Plaines has by far the most named-restaurant identities in the corpus.** Mian, RC Supply Co., Hugo's Frog Bar & Chop House, Coffee Spot, Taco Suerte, FLIPT — each with its own logo, type system, and photography register. This is the most "brand-mature" dining ecosystem across all five properties.
- **Des Plaines does multilingual work as a real practice.** Lunar New Year and Red Envelope DMs are produced in English, Chinese, and Vietnamese variants. None of the other properties show this in the corpus so far. This is genuine community-distinct creative.
- **Des Plaines has more named platforms than any other property,** including Aim for More, Scoreboard Scramble, Big Top Pops, Rush to $1 Million, April Showers Drawing, Clean Out the Closet, Red Envelope Giveaway, Cinco de Mayo Slot Play, Derby Drawing, Mother's Day Slot Play, Diverse Supplier civic work. Many have custom logos.
- **The DP voice signature is recurring puns where the pun connects to the offer mechanic:**
  - "Cake, candles and a lucky year" — birthday + wishing
  - "Love at first deal" — Valentine's + table cards
  - "Love at first spin" — Valentine's + slot reels
  - "Cloudy with a chance to win" — April Showers + weather idiom
  - "Power up your play" — VIP gift = portable charger
  - "We cooked up special gifts for you" / "Gifts are a recipe for success" — cookware giveaway
  - "Win! Place! Show!" — Kentucky Derby + horse-race terminology
  - "Play for points. Race for a million." — points race + Rush to $1M
  - "Treat yourself" — gift-self-care register
- **Recurring brand voice phrases that appear without seasonal hook (the workhorses):**
  - "Thrills await." / "Don't keep the thrills waiting."
  - "Weekends are for spinning!" / "Let's get the weekend spinning!"
  - "Rewards await." (tier work)
  - "Rewards bursting with bonuses." (points)
  - "Pick your day to play."
  - "Delicious flavors await." (Mian)
  - "Rewards you can taste." (RC Supply)
- **Des Plaines is the only property in the corpus showing genuine corporate / civic work** (Diverse Supplier ads, "Diversity equals success.").
- **Des Plaines = pun-driven copy, photography-first visuals, strong restaurant brand portfolio, multilingual when relevant.** The voice is in the words much more than at Pittsburgh.
